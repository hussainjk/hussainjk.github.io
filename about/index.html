About Me

I am currently a full-time compiler developer at the IBM Toronto software lab, and I’m a graduating undergraduate student in mathematics at the University of Toronto. I am blind, which you can read more about in my disability statement. In the upcoming year, I will be attending the University of Cambridge, where I will be completing Part III of the Mathematical Tripos.

Math

I am broadly interested in algebraic and arithmetic geometry, representation theory, and homotopy theory. I also enjoy making computers do interesting math.

  • Supervised by Ila Varma, I am doing a project on computing the average tortion of class groups of cubic number fields.
  • Supervised by Giulio Tiozzo, I am doing a project on random walks on Lamplighter groups.
  • I am currently participating in the Adjoint School on applied category theory.
  • I will be participating in the PCMI 2021 Undergraduate Summer School on quadratic forms, Milnor K-theory, and arithmetic.
