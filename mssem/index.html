Learning Seminar On Mirror Theory

Fall 2020

We are running an online learning seminar at the University of Toronto on mirror symmetry,.

organization

The seminar is being led by Waleed Qaisar. We meet every Monday at 9 am on zoom. If you would like to join, then please contact Waleed for the meeting password.

Outline

We will start by following this Clay monograph by Hori et al. The initial aim will be to finish Part 2 as quickly as possible, but not quicker. Even if this is the only thing we do, we should be able to declare the seminar a success.

If you are familiar with manifolds and differential forms, you can probably gain enough through a casual read to suffice for the first meeting.

Depending on how this goes, there is a plan to start another (disjoint), more advanced seminar on Automorphic Forms, Shimura Varieties, and the Langlands program.

Prerequisites

There are no formal prerequisites as such, but you can look through the Clay monograph and pick up whatever is required from Part 1. We will be going through Part 2 of the monograph in the seminar, and if it becomes apparent that some mathematical prerequisite is not understood by most people in the seminar, we might introduce it from Part 1 of the monograph. If wanted, we can recommend additional resources for the mathematical structures involved. On the physics side, not much is assumed besides some familiarity with quantum mechanics - we will adjust pace accordingly if there are holes to be filled.

sessions

Week 1 - Monday August 31

The tentative plan is to start with Part 2, Chapter 8 and Chapter 9 of the Clay Proceedings monograph. Please read the introduction before Part 1, and Chapter 8 (and if possible, 9) of Part 2 before the first session. It would be good to acquaint yourself with the relevant mathematical structures coming up here, namely vector bundles, principal bundles, connections, etc.

Week 2 - Sunday September 7

We will be going through chapter 9 of the monograph.
